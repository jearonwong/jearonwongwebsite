## 1. Core Diagnosis

The current design risk is not merely “outdated UI” or “weak modernity.”
The deeper risk is: the site reads as a structured publication / knowledge surface, rather than an unmistakable personal brand surface.

This brief exists to shift the site from:
- knowledge-index species
- literary-editorial species
- generic founder-site species
- generic AI product-site species

into:
> **AI-era Protocol Architect brand species**

---

## 2. Species Definition

### 2.1 The Site Must Feel Like
- a sharp personal headquarters
- a high-signal protocol/systems thinker’s public front door
- a modern AI-era founder-editorial surface
- a person with a defined category and thesis
- a site built around real intellectual and engineering gravity

**Core species keywords:** protocol-grade, founder-grade, systems-native, editorial-tech, high-contrast, premium, intentional, current, memorable, controlled

### 2.2 The Site Must NOT Feel Like
- a legacy personal blog
- a literary essay homepage
- a soft editorial journal
- a generic founder landing page
- a VC-style startup template
- a generic AI SaaS homepage
- a component demo with content inserted later
- a knowledge archive with a person appended to it

---

## 3. First-Fold Species Contract

The first fold must establish: 1. the person, 2. the thesis, 3. the visual memory anchor, 4. the immediate proof signal

**Required first-fold emotional output:**
- “This is a real person with category-defining intent.”
- “This person is not just writing; he is defining a systems layer.”
- “This site belongs to the AI/protocol/systems era.”
- “There is real proof immediately available.”

**Forbidden first-fold emotional output:**
- “This is a thoughtful blogger.”
- “This is a literary/publishing person.”
- “This is another AI startup landing page.”
- “This is a vague personal homepage.”
- “This is a content library first, person second.”

---

## 4. Visual Species Shift — Mandatory Changes

### 4.1 Species Shift in Background Model
The site must shift away from beige-paper mood, soft editorial warmth, muted literary atmosphere.
The site must move toward: dark identity zone, cool reading zone, stronger contrast between authority and reading surfaces.

### 4.2 Species Shift in Typography
The type system must imply: architected authority, structured intelligence, technical command, contemporary founder-grade precision.
**Rules:**
- Sans must dominate the homepage identity.
- Serif may survive only as a secondary editorial trace.
- Monospace may survive only as a systems signal layer.
*Species test:* If the typography makes the homepage feel like an essay journal before it feels like a category-defining systems thinker, it fails.

### 4.3 Species Shift in Portrait Function
The portrait area must stop behaving like a “designed placeholder” and begin behaving like a *brand anchor*.
It must hold visual gravity equal to the thesis block. A portrait fallback is not a placeholder, it is a temporary identity engine.

### 4.4 Species Shift in Card Logic
Cards may exist, but the site must not be card-led. The page must be driven primarily by: large visual fields, large typographic authority, major rhythm changes, controlled asymmetry, fewer, more decisive surfaces.

## 5. Homepage Species Architecture

The homepage must no longer read like a sequence of sections.
It must read like:
- **brand declaration**
- **systems proposition**
- **proof exposure**
- **thought platform**

### 5.1 Locked experiential order
1. Person
2. Thesis
3. Immediate Proof Signal
4. Signature Ideas
5. Why Now
6. Expanded Proof
7. Featured Writing
8. Playbooks
9. Quiet Close

---

## 6. Hero Species Rules
### 6.1 Hero must function as a declaration, not a welcome section
It must feel like a statement of authority, a category claim, a visual manifesto.

### 6.2 Left side rules
Must create name, thesis, and category memory. It must not become explanatory or overlong.

### 6.3 Right side rules
Must create tension, gravity, memory, identity. It must not become decorative symmetry or card filler.

---

## 7. Narrative Band Species Rules
The “Why Now” section must stop behaving like explanatory prose and become a *pressure band*.
It must feel like the industry turning point, a statement of inevitability.

---

## 8. Proof Species Rules
The proof chain must not feel like a “projects section.”
It must feel like: real artifacts, engineering gravity, evidence of category-building work.

---

## 9. Navigation Species Rules
Top navigation must stop communicating completeness/archive-ness.
It must communicate focus and confidence.
Preferred: Essays, Ideas, Playbooks, Projects, About.

---

## 10. Section Rhythm Species Rules
The homepage must contain *visible hierarchy shocks*.
Forbidden rhythm: same-height sections, same-density, same-card-count, same-surface treatment all the way down.

---

## 11. Archetype Rejection Table
An implementation fails if a reasonable viewer could say:
- “This looks like a modernized personal blog.”
- “This looks like a thoughtful essayist / publication person.”
- “This looks like a typical founder profile website.”
- “This looks like another AI product landing page.”
- “This is a content system first, and a person second.”

---

## 12. Species Recognition Tests
12.1 Person Recognition Test: Can the visitor remember Jearon Wong as a distinct person immediately?
12.2 Category Recognition Test: Can the visitor identify him as a protocol / systems / AI-era thinker?
12.3 Thesis Recognition Test: Can the visitor explain what he is defining in one sentence within seconds?
12.4 Proof Recognition Test: Can the visitor point to at least one proof artifact quickly?
12.5 Species Test: After seeing the first screen, would the visitor say “This site feels like a protocol architect’s headquarters”?

---

## 13. Implementation Red Lines
Forbidden: preserving beige mood, card-led surface, portrait as decorative card, serif-led feel, equal-weight hierarchy, generic founder visuals.

---

## 14. Compliance Rule
A design is compliant only if it is structurally aligned with the locked flow, visually aligned with the AI-era protocol architect species, perceptually distinct from the rejected archetypes, and memorable as a person-first brand surface.
